Contributions

  • The Global Biobank Engine was built using a modified version of the ExAC browser code-base (developed by the ExAC web development team), and an extended version of the IBD Exomes Browser developed by Manuel Rivas. The Engine development was led by Manuel Rivas. Special thanks to Chris Chang, developer of PLINK2, without whom it wolud not be possible to complete the work.
  • Manuel Rivas and Greg McInnes developed the coding (GWAS) page.
  • Greg McInnes and Adam Lavertu connected the submission page for MRP inference engine developed by Manuel A. Rivas and Matti Pirinen.
  • Greg McInnes and Yosuke Tanigawa developed Google account authentication.
  • Chris DeBoever developed the GBE Power App and the Genetic Correlation App. Both apps were conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.
  • Yosuke Tanigawa developed the variant (PheWAS) page and performed the Decomposition of Genetic Associations (DeGAs), COVID-19 HGI PRS-PheWAS analysis, and the phenome-wide application of sparse polygenic scores with contributions from colleagues and the supervision by Manuel. A. Rivas.
  • Matthew Aguirre developed the DeGAs polygenic risk modeling page. The DeGAs polygenic risk modeling page was conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.
  • Juliia Eve Olivieri developed the HLA Disease Map App. The HLA Disease Map App was conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.

Terms and Data Information

Terms of use

To cite the browser, please use: Global Biobank Engine, Stanford, CA (URL: http://gbe.stanford.edu) [date (month, year) accessed].

You can freely browse the association results.

Citation in publications

We request that any use of data obtained from the Global Biobank Engine be cited in publications using the following format:

  • Global Biobank Engine, Stanford, CA (URL: http://gbe.stanford.edu) [date (month, year) accessed].
  • G. McInnes, Y. Tanigawa, C. DeBoever, A. Lavertu, J. E. Olivieri, M. Aguirre, M. A. Rivas, Global Biobank Engine: enabling genotype-phenotype browsing for biobank summary statistics. Bioinformatics (2019). doi:10.1093/bioinformatics/bty999

We also request that any use of polygenic scores from the Global Biobank Engine be cited in publications using the following format:

  • Y. Tanigawa, J. Qian, G. R. Venkataraman, J. M. Justesen, R. Li, R. Tibshirani, T. Hastie, M. A. Rivas, Significant Sparse Polygenic Risk Scores across 813 traits in UK Biobank. PLOS Genet. 18(3), e1010105 (2022). doi:10.1371/journal.pgen.1010105
